2028 — GCC Expansion (Conditional) If Year 1 Hits Plan
The Trigger
GCC expansion only activates if Dubai Year 1 hits the financial model: 15.4M AED revenue, 9M AED EBITDA, 400+ members. If the numbers are proven, the playbook is validated and replicable. Capital for expansion comes from either retained profits, private investors, or a VC round — decision made based on how fast we want to move.
Target Market — Riyadh, KSA
  • Saudi Vision 2030 driving sports investment
  • Rapidly growing padel market
  • High-income population + govt backing
  • Model: owned or JV with local partner
  • Timeline: Q2 2028 open
Target Market — Abu Dhabi, UAE
  • Same legal + regulatory environment as Dubai
  • Strong demand, limited premium supply
  • Potential Yas Island or Saadiyat partnership
  • Model: owned second UAE location
  • Timeline: Q3 2028 open
Target Market — Kuwait / Bahrain
  • Smaller markets but very high HNW density
  • Ideal for franchise model (lower capex)
  • Local franchise partner preferred
  • App and equipment brand arrive first
  • Timeline: Franchise Q4 2028
Fernando Belasteguín Partnership Strategic · Handle Carefully
Belasteguín — 16 years world #1, the GOAT of padel — is the most powerful brand association available in this sport globally. His name opens doors in Spain, Latin America, the GCC, and anywhere padel is growing. This partnership, done right, is a differentiator no competitor can replicate.
Recommended Structure
  • Avoid: 5% of total revenue — too expensive at scale (3.4M AED/year by Year 3)
  • Preferred: 3–5% equity stake — aligns incentives, impresses investors
  • Alternative: flat $200K/year + 1% of club revenue only
  • His name on the club + academy, not necessarily on all verticals
  • Check HEAD racket deal before any equipment brand conversation
What He Brings
  • Global padel credibility — instant legitimacy
  • Present at Dubai opening → massive PR + media
  • Access to his professional network (tours, sponsors)
  • Brand name: "Belasteguín Academy at [Club Name]"
  • Potential to attract top coaches trained under him

Equipment Brand Strategy Competing with NOX · Babolat · Wilson · Adidas
The market is crowded but none of the existing brands own a club network. That is the edge — equipment sold inside the clubs we operate, to members who identify with the brand, at 65%+ gross margin with no retailer taking a cut. The brand is born inside the club, not on a shelf.
Positioning
  • Premium, club-native brand — not mass market
  • Identity-driven like Lululemon in yoga, On Running in running
  • Padel-only — not tennis crossover like Wilson or Babolat
  • Bela signature line if partnership includes equipment rights
  • UAE / GCC designed — Arabic branding variant
Year 1 Product Range
  • 3 racket SKUs: Entry (600 AED) · Pro (900 AED) · Signature (1,400 AED)
  • Padel bag + accessories
  • Club apparel: polo, shorts, cap, socks
  • Sold in-club + DTC online + future retail
  • Manufactured in Spain / Portugal (premium origin)
